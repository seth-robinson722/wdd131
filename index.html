Texas

Texas occupies a vast $268,597$ square mile territory in the South Central United States, characterized by a diverse progression of landscapes ranging from coastal pine marshes to arid western deserts. The state’s geography is defined by four major physical regions: the Gulf Coastal Plains, the Interior Lowlands, the Great Plains, and the Basin and Range Province. Its economy is a global powerhouse, anchored by a massive energy sector, advanced aerospace engineering, and a rapidly expanding technology corridor known as the "Silicon Hills." The climate varies significantly across its breadth, transitioning from humid subtropical conditions in the east to semi-arid and mountainous environments in the west. Texas also maintains a unique cultural identity shaped by its history as an independent republic and its position as a major crossroads for international trade and migration.
